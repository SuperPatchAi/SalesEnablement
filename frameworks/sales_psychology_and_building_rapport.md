## Sales Training // How to Build Rapport with ANYONE // Andy Elliott

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r3st2GFOO_A](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r3st2GFOO_A)

This video focuses on building immediate rapport and relationships with strangers to improve sales outcomes. The core idea is that people buy from those they like, trust, and who genuinely care.

---

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
**Main Topic:** Mastering a Stranger / Building Instant Rapport and Genuine Connections

**Technique:** Proactive, Positive, and Purposeful Engagement to Establish Common Ground

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework 1: Intentional & Positive Approach**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Assume a Great Attitude & Smile:** "If I was going to walk up to you, right, and I have a great attitude... and I'm smiling, I'm going to make you smile."
    2.  **Visual Engagement:** "Show some smiling eyes, smile with your teeth."
    3.  **Personal Presentation:** "Make sure your breath don't smell like a sandwich, you got all the crusties out of your eyes, your beard shaved nice and tight, make sure that your hair is trimmed, you look clean, you look presentable."
    4.  **Physical Connection:** Initiate a handshake, high-five, or fist pound. "Every single time... The second you grab my hand, we connect. It's over."
    5.  **Be in a "Flow State":** Be natural, not "weird to talk to." Be enthusiastic.

*   **Psychology/Principle:**
    *   **Mirroring/Contagion:** A genuine smile and positive attitude are contagious, prompting a similar positive response from the other person.
    *   **First Impressions:** Good hygiene and a polished appearance build immediate credibility and approachability.
    *   **Human Connection:** People are "built to connect," and physical touch (appropriate) reinforces that connection.
    *   **Setting the Tone:** The salesperson is responsible for initiating and leading a positive interaction. "They're not having a great day because there's nothing to have a great day about, and that's your problem. You're the one not doing your job."

**Framework 2: Speaking with Familiarity & Genuine Care**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Speak as if Already Known:** "Speak with familiarity. Don't ever speak to someone like you don't know them."
    2.  **Focus on Liking:** Recognize that simply knowing about the product isn't enough. "You've got to close them on liking you."
    3.  **Proactive Conversation Starters:** Don't wait for an opening; actively look for and create reasons to start a genuine conversation.
    4.  **Identify Common Ground:** "You must, you must find something to start a conversation about." This involves observing "something on their vehicle, something that they're wearing, or something that they've said within a quick minute or two."
    5.  **Show Trustworthiness:** "Show people that you care, show people that you're the trusted advisor."

*   **Psychology/Principle:**
    *   **Relatability:** People are more comfortable with those who seem familiar or relatable, breaking down initial guardedness.
    *   **Emotional Connection:** Genuine interest and care build a deeper emotional connection than mere transactional interaction. "People don't care about how much you know until they know how much you care."
    *   **Trust as a Foundation:** Establishing oneself as a "trusted advisor" positions the salesperson as a helpful expert rather than just a seller.

**Framework 3: Practice and Competence**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Consistent Practice:** "Practice and repetition is the mother of skill."
    2.  **Study and Learn:** Continuously educate yourself to improve. "Educating yourself to be the best..."
    3.  **Develop Competence:** Focus on becoming excellent at your craft.
    4.  **Build Confidence:** Competence naturally leads to confidence. "...competence creates confidence."

*   **Psychology/Principle:**
    *   **Mastery:** Deliberate practice is essential for skill development, even for "introverts."
    *   **Self-Efficacy:** When you know you are good at something, you exhibit more confidence, which is attractive and reassuring to prospects.
    *   **Authority:** Being knowledgeable and competent positions you as an authority, further solidifying trust.

### 3. EXACT SCRIPTS & WORD TRACKS

*   **Opening Lines (General):**
    *   "Hey, how are you doing? Hope you have the best day of your life, how can I help you today?"
    *   "Hey, how are you doing?" (Said with a genuine smile and welcoming face)
    *   (After initial engagement) "Hey, by the way, what's your name? My name's Andy, how are you doing?"

*   **Discovery Questions (Building Common Ground):**
    *   "Where are you from?"
    *   (If you spot a commonality, e.g., a sports team hat) "That's awesome, I think I've seen you around here. What brings you out today?"

*   **Relationship Building Phrases:**
    *   (To a veteran) "Hey, thank you so much for serving."
    *   (Observing family) "Is this your wife right here? Oh, that's your daughter? That's great! Man, you guys are awesome. These are the kiddos? Dang, you guys got a model family!"
    *   "Right out the gate, man, I'm having a good time, I'm having fun, I'm taking that damn wall and I'm ripping it down." (Expressing your positive state.)

*   **Follow-up Messages (Call to Action for the Speaker's Program):**
    *   "Shoot me a text message right now: 918-210-0254."
    *   "Say, 'Hey Andy, my name's Bobby, I was watching your videos, can you tell me about Project 500?'"

### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING

The video doesn't provide specific scripts for handling objections like "I'm not interested" or "It's too expensive." Instead, its approach to "objection handling" is **proactive and preventative**. By mastering the art of connecting, building rapport, and establishing trust *before* a product pitch, the salesperson aims to reduce the likelihood of hard objections.

*   **Core Principle:** By getting prospects to "like you" and "know you care," you create an environment where objections are less likely to arise as barriers and more likely to be questions of clarification.
*   **Response to a "Cold" Prospect:** The speaker states, "If you're like reserved or you feel weird, like, then I feel weird. And by the way, the colder you are, the colder I am." This implies that the salesperson's role is to *break* the coldness through their own warmth and initiative, rather than react to it.

### 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  **You Set the Tone:** Your attitude, smile, and proactive engagement directly influence the prospect's receptiveness. Be the one to set a positive, welcoming, and familiar pace for interaction.
2.  **Connect First, Sell Second:** Prioritize building genuine rapport and demonstrating care. People are more likely to buy from those they like and trust, regardless of product knowledge initially.
3.  **Seek Common Ground Everywhere:** Train your eyes and ears to spot shared interests (hobbies, family, military service, clothing, bumper stickers) to quickly initiate meaningful conversations.
4.  **Competence Fuels Confidence:** Continuously educate yourself and practice your sales skills. The more competent you become, the more confident you'll be, making you more effective and trustworthy.
5.  **Small Actions, Big Impact:** Simple gestures like a genuine smile, a direct greeting, and asking a personal question can lead to engagement and break down barriers with strangers.

### 6. SUPER PATCH APPLICATION

Applying these techniques to selling Super Patch wellness products in a direct sales/network marketing context:

1.  **The Approach - Be a Walking Ambassador of Wellness:**
    *   **Pre-Approach Mindset:** Before meeting anyone (in person or virtually), get into a "flow state." Be genuinely enthusiastic about the transformative potential of Super Patch. Think about how you are helping people, not just selling patches.
    *   **Personal Presentation:** Wear a patch visibly (e.g., on your wrist like a watch, or near your collarbone). This subtly introduces the product and serves as an organic conversation starter. Maintain a vibrant, energetic appearance that aligns with the wellness message.
    *   **Greeting:** Approach with a beaming smile and an open, friendly demeanor.
        *   "Hey, how are you doing today? You look incredibly energetic!" (If they have a patch on) "Are you a fellow Super Patch user? That's awesome!"
        *   "My name is [Your Name], it's great to meet you! Hope you're having an amazing day."

2.  **Building Common Ground & Genuine Connection:**
    *   **Observation:** Look for cues. Do they have a sports team logo? A marathon shirt? A tattoo related to a hobby or family? Are they carrying a gym bag or a coffee cup from a local cafe?
    *   **Conversation Starter:** Use these observations to initiate a **genuine** chat.
        *   "I love your running shoes – are you a runner? My [friend/client] who also runs swears by these little neurotech patches for recovery and energy. Ever heard of them?" (Subtly introducing the product *after* finding common ground).
        *   "That's a beautiful [sports team/college] shirt! Are you from around here? What do you do for work/fun?" (Listen for health-related pain points or aspirations).
        *   "I see your tattoo [mention specific detail, e.g., 'of a mountain range'] – that's really unique! Do you love hiking? How do you keep your energy up for those long treks?"
    *   **Show You Care:** Actively listen to their responses. Ask follow-up questions that demonstrate genuine interest in *them*, not just their potential as a customer.
        *   "Wow, it sounds like you have a really demanding schedule. How do you manage to stay focused/get good sleep with all that going on?" (This opens the door to solutions).
        *   "I hear you, [mention their pain point, e.g., 'aches after a long day']. Many people face similar challenges, and finding simple, effective solutions can be a game-changer."

3.  **Establishing Trust & Competence:**
    *   **Trusted Advisor:** Don't immediately launch into a product dump. Position yourself as someone who understands wellness and can offer solutions.
        *   "As a wellness advisor, I'm always looking for innovative ways to help people optimize their health. I've personally seen incredible results with [mention a specific patch, e.g., 'the Energy Patch'] for people who need that extra boost without caffeine crashes."
    *   **Confidence from Competence:** Understand the science behind the patches (Vibrotactile Technology, neurological pathways) so you can explain it simply and confidently without jargon.
        *   "It's really fascinating how our bodies work, and these patches leverage that. They don't put anything *into* your body, but rather use a unique pattern to stimulate your nervous system, kind of like a QR code for your brain. It helps your body rebalance itself."
    *   **Practice Your Story:** Have a concise, compelling personal story about how Super Patch has helped you or a close client, delivered with conviction.
        *   "Before I found these, I used to [mention your past struggle, e.g., 'wake up tired no matter how much I slept'], but since using [mention specific patch], I've noticed [specific benefit, e.g., 'a significant improvement in deep sleep and I wake up refreshed']. It's been a total game-changer for me."

## How to Gain Trust & Build Rapport Faster | 5 Minute Sales Training

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yDcfvjAsm8I](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yDcfvjAsm8I)

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
**Main Topic:** Building Trust and Rapport Faster
**Technique:** Optimizing the "Critical 12 Seconds" of First Impressions

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework 1: The Critical 12 Seconds**
This framework emphasizes the extreme importance of the initial moments of interaction with a customer. It's broken down into two parts:

*   **5 Seconds Before (The Customer Walks Through Your Door):**
    1.  **Be on your feet and moving.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** "Movement = Energy." When a customer sees you sitting down, it often leaves a negative or neutral impression (e.g., "What? Can't you see I'm busy?"). Being on your feet signals readiness, enthusiasm, and a proactive approach. It helps project "positive energy" which is crucial for a good first impression.
    2.  **Mental preparation/Personal Mantra.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** Even if you've had a bad day or bad news, the customer deserves your "very best self." A personal mantra helps shift your mindset to a positive and enthusiastic state, ensuring you project "positive energy" rather than negative or neutral energy. Examples given: "It's time to sell a home!", "It's Show Time!", or "It's Time to Change Someone's World!"

*   **7 Seconds After (The Customer Walks Through Your Door):**
    1.  **Retire generic greetings.** Avoid phrases like "Hi, how can I help you?" or "What brought you out today?"
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** These greetings are common and don't make the customer feel special or welcomed. They contribute to a transactional, rather than relational, interaction.
    2.  **Make the customer feel welcome and special.** They are "guests to our home."
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** This creates an immediate positive emotional connection, differentiating your approach from competitors.
    3.  **Express gratitude for their visit.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** Shows appreciation and respect for their time, reinforcing the feeling of being valued.
    4.  **Introduce yourself, ask their name, and use it.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** Personalization and remembering names are fundamental to building rapport and showing genuine interest. It makes the interaction feel more human and less robotic.
    5.  **Engage in friendly conversation before business.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** This builds rapport and shows you're interested in them as a person, not just a transaction. It allows for a more natural connection and makes them feel at ease.
    6.  **Maintain good facial posture and body language, and make good eye contact.**
        *   **Psychology/Principle:** Non-verbal cues are powerful in conveying sincerity, confidence, and trustworthiness. Open body language and eye contact signal honesty and engagement.

**Framework 2: Coffee Worthy**
*   **Definition:** "Within a couple minutes worth of time, your customer should like you and trust you enough that they would be willing to go and have a cup of coffee with you."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** This concept encapsulates the ultimate goal of rapid rapport and trust building. If a customer feels comfortable enough to share a casual coffee, it signifies a strong personal connection beyond just a business transaction. This level of likability and trust makes them "more inclined to slow down and answer your questions, more inclined to allow you to influence and persuade me, and more inclined to share my story with you."

### 3. EXACT SCRIPTS & WORD TRACKS

*   **Opening Lines (to be *retired*):**
    *   "Hi, how can I help you?"
    *   "What brought you out today?"

*   **Personal Mantras (for mental preparation):**
    *   "It's time to sell a home!"
    *   "It's Show Time!"
    *   "It's Time to Change Someone's World!"

*   **During the first 7 seconds after interaction begins:**
    *   (No exact scripts for *new* greetings are provided, but the emphasis is on being welcoming, grateful, introducing yourself, asking and using their name, and engaging in friendly conversation before business.)

*   **The Power of Story (after building rapport):**
    *   "If you know my story, as a buyer, you will know me so well that I will tell you how to sell me a home."

### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING
The video discusses the *prevention* of negative first impressions and the *difficulty* of overcoming them, rather than specific objection handling scripts.

*   **Common Situations that could lead to a negative impression:**
    *   Appointment cancellations.
    *   Lost contracts.
    *   Dealing with an angry customer on the phone.

*   **Recommended Approach (Prevention/Mindset):**
    *   Acknowledge that bad news happens.
    *   Regardless of personal/previous professional setbacks, "The show must go on" and the next person "deserves your very best self."
    *   This implies a need to compartmentalize and bring positive energy to each new interaction, rather than carrying over negativity.

### 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  **First Impressions are Lightning Fast:** Customers make judgments about trustworthiness, competence, and likability within a fraction of a second, and these initial judgments are very hard to change.
2.  **Prioritize Positive Energy:** Be on your feet and moving *before* the customer enters your space. Use a personal mantra to ensure you project positive energy and enthusiasm, avoiding neutral or negative vibes.
3.  **Make Greetings Personal & Welcoming:** Retire generic greetings. Instead, express gratitude for their visit, introduce yourself, ask and use their name, and initiate friendly conversation before diving into business.
4.  **Be "Coffee Worthy":** Aim to build enough rapport and trust in the first few minutes that a customer would genuinely want to sit down and have a casual cup of coffee with you.
5.  **Likability Fuels Trust and Openness:** When customers like and trust you, they are more likely to share their needs, ask questions, be influenced, and ultimately, be ready to buy.

### 6. SUPER PATCH APPLICATION

Applying these techniques to selling Super Patch wellness products (e.g., pain relief, energy, sleep patches) in a direct sales/network marketing context:

1.  **Prepare for Every "Meeting" (Online or In-Person):**
    *   **5 Seconds Before:** If you're doing an online demo or meeting, ensure you're energized. Stand up before the call, take a deep breath, perhaps use a mantra like "I'm about to help someone feel better today!" or "It's time to share the gift of wellness!" If it's an in-person pop-up or one-on-one, be visible, approachable, and actively engaged, not sitting passively.
    *   **Why it works for Super Patch:** Super Patch often deals with personal wellness challenges (pain, sleep issues). A highly energized and positive consultant immediately makes the customer feel hopeful and enthusiastic about potential solutions.

2.  **Craft a Warm, Engaging Welcome:**
    *   **Instead of:** "Hi, can I tell you about Super Patch?" or "What kind of pain are you in?" (too direct/clinical).
    *   **Try:** "Welcome! It's so great to meet you! My name is [Your Name]. What brings you out today/to our call? I'm curious to hear what you're hoping to learn or achieve." (Express gratitude for their time, introduce yourself, ask their name, use it).
    *   **Friendly Conversation:** Start with a brief, genuine non-product related comment (e.g., "I love your [accessory/shirt]," "How's your day going outside of this meeting?"). Then transition by saying something like, "Many people come to me looking for natural ways to improve their well-being, whether it's managing discomfort or boosting their energy. What's on your mind today, [Customer Name]?"
    *   **Why it works for Super Patch:** People are often vulnerable when discussing health issues. A warm, personal greeting immediately puts them at ease and opens them up to sharing.

3.  **Project "Coffee Worthy" Qualities:**
    *   **Be Friendly:** Smile, use open body language, and maintain positive eye contact (even virtually).
    *   **Be Interested/Interesting:** Ask open-ended questions about their lifestyle, challenges, and what they've tried before. Listen actively. Share a *brief*, relatable personal anecdote if appropriate, showing you understand their struggle without dominating the conversation.
    *   **Be a Good Listener:** Really hear their pain points and desired outcomes. Let them talk more than you do in the initial stages. Reflect their feelings to show empathy (e.g., "That sounds really frustrating," or "I can imagine how much better you'd feel if you could...").
    *   **Why it works for Super Patch:** Building this personal connection makes the customer trust that you genuinely care about their well-being, not just making a sale. They'll be more likely to share details about their health journey, which allows you to better recommend the right Super Patch products. If they like and trust you, they'll see you as a guide, not just a seller.

4.  **Leverage Trust for Deep Discovery:**
    *   Once rapport is built, the customer will be "more inclined to share their story with you." This is your opportunity to understand their health challenges deeply.
    *   **Example:** "Thank you for sharing, [Customer Name]. It sounds like [reiterate their problem, e.g., 'your knee pain really impacts your ability to enjoy walks with your dog']. How long has that been an issue for you? What have you tried so far?"
    *   **Why it works for Super Patch:** This in-depth understanding allows you to tailor your Super Patch recommendations perfectly, making the solution feel highly personalized and effective, further cementing trust. They're telling you "how to sell them" by revealing their needs.

## The most powerful sales psychology framework?

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5npmlzv8Y1g](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5npmlzv8Y1g)

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE

The primary sales concept/technique taught is **The Hierarchy of Commitment**. This framework explains what people value most beyond just money, moving up a pyramid of commitment from basic monetary exchange to deeper emotional and identity-based values.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

The core framework is **The Hierarchy of Commitment**, structured as a pyramid with **Money** at the base and **Identity** at the apex. Each level represents a higher form of commitment and value beyond the one below it.

1.  **Framework/Technique Name:** Money
    *   **Step-by-step process:** (Implied, not explicit steps for sales technique, but a foundational understanding)
        1.  Recognize money as the lowest form of commitment.
        2.  Understand money as a store and measure of value.
        3.  View money as a resource of opportunity, not an outcome.
    *   **Psychology/Principle:** People exchange money for things they *value*. The amount they spend is proportional to the perceived value. Money itself is not the ultimate goal but a means to acquire what is valued. Those who hoard money without exchanging it for deeper values are often unhappy.

2.  **Framework/Technique Name:** Convenience (exchanged for Time)
    *   **Step-by-step process:** (Applied to sales)
        1.  Demonstrate how your solution makes the customer's current job easier.
        2.  Identify how many of the customer's needs your solution can meet at once.
        3.  Ensure you are easy to deal with as a salesperson.
    *   **Psychology/Principle:** Money is renewable, but time is not. People will happily exchange money for more time and ease. In sales, being convenient (making processes faster, more efficient, reducing friction) makes you and your solution more valuable. "One throat to choke" is a key concept here – offering a comprehensive solution to multiple needs.

3.  **Framework/Technique Name:** Emotional Safety (exchanged for Energy)
    *   **Step-by-step process:** (Applied to sales)
        1.  Strive to enter the customer's trusted personal network.
        2.  Observe if customers are open with insights into their business or guarded with answers (this indicates their level of trust).
        3.  If you haven't established trust yourself, leverage someone else in their network who has (e.g., a partner).
    *   **Psychology/Principle:** Emotions are "energy in motion." People will happily exchange time and money for positive emotional energy. The primary positive emotion to leverage in sales is **Trust**. Customers want to feel safe and confident in their decisions. Trust is built through consistent, trustworthy actions, leading to familiarity and a strong personal brand. Lack of trust leads to guardedness and resistance.

4.  **Framework/Technique Name:** Identity (reinforced with Status)
    *   **Step-by-step process:** (Applied to sales)
        1.  Understand your customer's professional and personal agenda in the outcome of the solution.
        2.  Demonstrate how your solution makes them look good to those they are accountable to (bosses, colleagues, family).
        3.  Be charming and subtly reinforce their ego/self-perception.
    *   **Psychology/Principle:** Identity is the highest form of commitment. People are deeply committed to who they perceive themselves to be (their identity) and how they are perceived by others (their status). This identity is reinforced by titles, clothing, education, luxuries, and success. By helping customers achieve their professional and personal agendas, and by subtly reinforcing their status and making them look good, you tap into their highest form of commitment.

### 3. EXACT SCRIPTS & WORD TRACKS

The video focuses more on strategic thinking and questions to consider rather than exact scripts. However, it provides clear guidance on *what* to communicate.

*   **General Sales Principle:** "SALES = VALUE > PRICE" (0:49)
*   **Convenience:**
    *   "How does your solution make their job easier than current?" (10:31)
    *   "How many of their needs can you meet at once with your solution?" (10:44)
    *   "How easy are you to deal with?" (11:03)
    *   *"The more convenient you are in solution and service, the more valuable you become."* (3:30)
*   **Emotional Safety (Trust):**
    *   "Are you in the customers' trusted personal network?" (11:23)
    *   "Are they open with insight into the business or are they guarded with answers?" (11:34)
    *   "If you haven't established trust, has someone else e.g. partner?" (11:56)
    *   *"We develop trust through consistent trustworthy actions to develop our personal brand."* (6:24)
*   **Status (Identity):**
    *   "What is your customers' Professional & Personal Agenda in this outcome?" (12:17)
    *   "Are you making them look good to who they're accountable to?" (12:31)
    *   "Are you charming & 'stroking the ego'?" (12:49)
    *   *"Customers give us their money, time & energy when we reinforce their Identity."* (9:09)

### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING

The video does not explicitly discuss specific objections or their responses. However, the entire framework is designed to *prevent* objections related to price, time, risk, and perceived value by building higher levels of commitment.

**Implied Objection Prevention:**
*   **"It's too expensive" (Money objection):** Prevented by demonstrating value (convenience, emotional safety, status) that far outweighs the price, making the purchase a "no-brainer investment."
*   **"I don't have time" (Time/Convenience objection):** Prevented by showing how your solution *saves* them time and makes their life more convenient.
*   **"I don't trust you/your product" (Emotional Safety objection):** Prevented by establishing yourself as a "Trusted Advisor" through consistent, trustworthy actions and demonstrating positive emotional energy.
*   **"This doesn't fit my goals/I don't need this" (Identity/Status objection):** Prevented by deeply understanding their professional and personal agendas and showing how your solution elevates their status and reinforces their desired identity.

### 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  **Value Beyond Price:** Sales is about demonstrating that the value derived from your solution far exceeds its price. To do this effectively, understand what people value *more* than money.
2.  **The Hierarchy of Commitment:** Recognize that people commit at different levels: Money (lowest), then Time/Convenience, then Energy/Emotional Safety (Trust), and finally Identity/Status (highest). Focus on climbing this hierarchy.
3.  **Convenience Sells:** Make your solution and yourself as easy and efficient to deal with as possible. Address multiple customer needs with a single, streamlined approach.
4.  **Build Trust through Emotional Safety:** Cultivate trust by being emotionally safe. This means consistent, trustworthy actions that build familiarity and rapport, making customers feel secure in their interactions and decisions.
5.  **Reinforce Identity and Status:** Understand your customer's professional and personal aspirations. Frame your solution as something that enhances their status, makes them look good to others, and aligns with their desired identity.

### 6. SUPER PATCH APPLICATION

Here's how the Hierarchy of Commitment can be applied to selling Super Patch wellness products:

1.  **Money (Value > Price):**
    *   **Focus:** Position the patch not as an expense, but as an *investment* in their overall well-being, health, and ability to live fully.
    *   **Script Idea:** "Instead of seeing the cost of Super Patch, consider it an investment in regaining your energy, achieving better sleep, or living pain-free. What value do you place on feeling truly optimal every day?"
    *   **Highlight:** Contrast the one-time (or subscription) cost with the ongoing expenses of temporary fixes, medications, or lost productivity due to health issues.

2.  **Convenience (Time):**
    *   **Focus:** Emphasize the unparalleled ease of use and time-saving aspects of the patches.
    *   **Script Idea:** "Imagine the time you'll reclaim not needing to remember multiple pills, make frequent doctor appointments, or deal with messy creams. With Super Patch, it's just peel, stick, and you're done. It integrates effortlessly into your busy life, giving you back precious time."
    *   **Highlight:** No complex routines, no side effects, discreet usage, portable. Show how it fits into their current lifestyle without disruption.

3.  **Emotional Safety (Energy/Trust):**
    *   **Focus:** Build trust by sharing genuine experiences, testimonials, and being empathetic to their struggles. Address any skepticism directly.
    *   **Script Idea (Building Trust):** "I understand if you're skeptical; many people are when they first hear about this innovative technology. But I've seen firsthand, both in my own life and with countless others, the profound positive impact. I'm here to answer all your questions and ensure you feel completely confident."
    *   **Script Idea (Positive Energy):** "Think about the emotional freedom that comes with reduced chronic pain, or the sense of peace from finally getting restorative sleep. That's the 'good energy' Super Patch can bring, allowing you to be more present and joyful."
    *   **Highlight:** Offer a trial period or money-back guarantee to mitigate perceived risk. Share the science behind the technology in an accessible way.

4.  **Identity (Status):**
    *   **Focus:** Connect the use of Super Patch to their desired self-image and how it enhances their status or ability to live out their values.
    *   **Script Idea (Professional/Personal Agenda):** "What kind of parent/partner/professional do you want to be? If you had more energy, imagine how much more engaged you could be with your family, or how much more you could achieve at work. Super Patch empowers you to embody that ideal self you strive for."
    *   **Script Idea (Stroking Ego/Status):** "You seem like someone who's always looking for cutting-edge solutions and takes a proactive approach to their health. This innovative technology is truly for people like you who are ahead of the curve in wellness."
    *   **Highlight:** Discuss how feeling great allows them to participate in activities they love (hobbies, sports), be a better role model for their children, or excel in their career without health limitations. It's about being seen as strong, healthy, and capable.

## The Latest Psychology Of Selling: All Of The New Data 2023

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=prMsgaVo3aQ](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=prMsgaVo3aQ)

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
The primary sales concept/technique being taught is **Data-Driven Sales Psychology**. It emphasizes leveraging modern insights and understanding human psychology to optimize sales interactions, moving away from outdated "guru" advice.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework 1: Benefit-Oriented Selling / Challenge-Solution Matching**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Understand exactly what's going on in *their* world.
    2.  Identify the key challenges *they're* facing.
    3.  Match those challenges with the *solutions* we offer.
    4.  Never really focusing too deeply on the *actual features* of what we offer.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "The data is unequivocal: salespeople that lead their sales conversations with the features of their offering are far less likely to actually close the deal." Prospects care about their problems and how a solution can alleviate them, not a list of features.

**Framework 2: Conversational Selling / Engaged Dialogue**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Engage in much more back-and-forth conversations.
    2.  Avoid monologues (either the salesperson talking too much or the prospect talking too much without interruption).
    3.  Keep the conversation "fully engaged" like a "ping-pong match."
    4.  Use "quick questions" to constantly go back and forth.
    5.  Catch up with them "almost like you're catching up with a friend."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "Top performing sales reps are having much more back and forth conversations in sales." This increases engagement, builds rapport, and makes the conversation feel more natural and productive.

**Framework 3: Paced Communication / Comfortable Conversation**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Consciously slow down the pace of the conversation.
    2.  Allow both the salesperson and the prospect to speak fewer words per minute.
    3.  Create a "comfortable conversation" with a "genuine back-and-forth."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "The data shows is that conversations by top performing salespeople are much slower in terms of the actual pace." A slower pace indicates comfort and trust, reducing perceived pressure and allowing for deeper understanding.

**Framework 4: Qualification / Mindset of Abundance**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Realize that "about half of prospects that you're going to come across are never going to be a fit."
    2.  Shift the goal from needing to "close every single person" to instead determining if the prospect is an "actual fit."
    3.  If they're not a fit, "move on."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** This changes the "psychology of how we're selling." Removing the pressure to close every deal allows the salesperson to be more genuine, focus on true fit, and not waste time on unqualified leads, thereby increasing overall efficiency and mental resilience.

**Framework 5: Gamification of Sales / Detachment from Outcome**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  View sales as "like a videogame."
    2.  Recognize that even if a sales interaction goes "horribly wrong," there are no severe real-world consequences (e.g., "they're not going to remember you, they're not going to come up to you at the supermarket and start yelling at you").
*   **Psychology/Principle:** This mindset removes pressure, allowing salespeople to be "mentally stronger" and feel "in control of any situation." It encourages experimentation and reduces anxiety associated with perceived failure.

**Framework 6: Authenticity & Enthusiasm / Emotional Contagion**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  You must be genuinely enjoying the fact that you're just connecting with another person.
    2.  Avoid being "cheesy and insincere."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Prospects can sense if you're not enjoying the conversation and will consequently "want to end that conversation." Genuine enjoyment and connection foster a positive atmosphere that encourages the prospect to stay engaged.

**Framework 7: Confidence & Poise / Overcoming Sales Anxiety**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  "Put your mind at ease" by internalizing that sales is a "game" (referencing Framework 5).
    2.  Recognize there's "no pressure to close this specific deal."
    3.  Focus on "just having a conversation" and enjoying it (referencing Framework 6).
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "Humans sense fear instantly." Our primal programming allows us to immediately sense fear (manifesting as twitching, voice trembling, etc.) in others. If a salesperson portrays fear, the prospect will know "right away," undermining trust and likelihood of success.

**Framework 8: Active Listening / Prospect-Centric Dialogue**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Engage prospects in conversations where "they're doing a lot of the talking."
    2.  Focus on fostering back-and-forth dialogue.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "People enjoy conversations in which they're fully engaged, in which they're doing a lot of the talking." This makes prospects feel heard, understood, and "so much more bought in to the ultimate solutions that we share at the end of that conversation."

**Framework 9: Problem-Solution Selling / Value Proposition Mapping**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  Understand the prospect's current situation (point A) and their desired outcome (point B).
    2.  Identify the "rocky road" or challenges that prevent them from reaching point B.
    3.  Demonstrate how *your solution* can help them get from point A to point B.
    4.  Ensure the Return on Investment (ROI) "sounds right" so the solution appears as a "total no-brainer."
    5.  Focus on the *end solution* you will create for them, not primarily on product features.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "If they believe that you can help them solve their challenges, your solution is going to seem like a total no-brainer." This is the "ultimate key" because prospects buy outcomes and solutions to their problems, not just products.

Salespeople need to shift their thinking to the "objective state" (the desired outcome or result). By immediately framing the conversation around results, you prevent objections related to price from becoming central. Jeremy emphasizes, "I always think I'm the one causing the objections, not the prospect."

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework:** Building Rapport with Anyone in Sales

**Step 1: Stop Trying To Mimic Them**
*   **Process:** Avoid physically mirroring or mimicking your prospect's movements, posture, or tone.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** The "old school" advice of mimicry is often transparent and doesn't build genuine rapport. True rapport comes from demonstrating genuine comfort, not imitation. People are naturally more comfortable around others who are also comfortable and authentic.

**Step 2: Show That You're At Ease**
*   **Process:** Cultivate and project a relaxed, comfortable demeanor during the sales conversation.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** This builds directly on the previous point. Your comfort level influences the prospect's comfort level. Nervousness in a salesperson (common for new sales reps) is easily detected and can make the prospect feel uneasy, breaking rapport.
*   **Actionable Tip:** Before a meeting, take deep breaths, relax, and visualize yourself feeling at ease in the conversation.

**Step 3: Take A Leadership Role**
*   **Process:** Guide the conversation and demonstrate expertise, rather than passively following the prospect's lead.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "Leadership does not mean dominance." Instead, it means providing clear direction and expertise. Prospects appreciate leadership, especially when they are uncertain about the process or solution. It builds trust and establishes you as a credible expert. Allowing the prospect to lead the conversation can make them uncomfortable and feel like you lack expertise, ultimately breaking rapport.
*   **Analogy:** Think of yourself like a doctor who leads a patient through a diagnosis.

**Step 4: Limit Your Time On Pleasantries**
*   **Process:** Keep small talk and pleasantries brief, ideally no more than a minute or so, before transitioning to the main purpose of the conversation.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** While some pleasantries are acceptable to break the ice, spending too much time on them is perceived as a "waste of time" by busy prospects. This can lead to discomfort and damage rapport, as it suggests you don't value their time or the importance of the meeting. Prospects want to get to the point.

**Step 5: Focus On Their Issues**
*   **Process:** Structure and "cater the entire conversation around them" – their challenges, goals, objectives, and specific issues.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "There is nothing that makes a person feel more important and more special and relevant than when we're focused on them and their issues." Most salespeople mistakenly focus on pitching their product, company, or themselves. Prospects, however, care most about *themselves* and their problems. This prospect-centric approach naturally builds the strongest and most profound level of rapport.

## The Psychology of Selling: 13 Steps to Selling that Work

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
The primary sales concept taught is **"The Psychology of Selling: The 13 Steps to Selling That Actually Works."** It focuses on understanding and leveraging human psychology to build rapport, uncover true needs, and guide prospects toward a natural buying decision without resorting to traditional, high-pressure sales tactics.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

The video outlines 13 steps for effective selling:

**1. Drop the enthusiasm**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Reduce overly excited or enthusiastic demeanor. Adopt a calm, genuine, and authentic tone.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "Reactance" – people resist being pushed. Over-enthusiasm can make you seem inauthentic, desperate, or like you're trying too hard to sell, which triggers resistance in prospects. Being real and genuine builds trust.

**2. They don't want the pitch**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Prioritize understanding the prospect's needs and concerns through conversation *before* presenting any solution or pitch.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** A common reason sales are lost is that the salesperson doesn't truly understand the prospect's needs. Prospects want to feel understood and engaged in a conversation about *their* situation, not lectured about a product. This builds rapport and ensures any eventual pitch is relevant.

**3. Pressure is a "No-No"**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Remove all pressure from the selling situation. Focus on asking questions to *determine if there's a fit*, rather than trying to persuade them.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Similar to "Reactance," putting pressure on a prospect makes them pull back and resist. A no-pressure approach allows them to feel in control and more open to genuine conversation.

**4. It's about them, not you**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Shift the entire conversation focus from your offerings to the prospect's world: their concerns, challenges, goals, and what matters most to them.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** "WIIFM" (What's In It For Me?). Prospects care about themselves and their problems, not your product or service. By focusing on *their* issues, you make the conversation valuable and engaging for them, leading to increased trust and willingness to open up.

**5. Get in their shoes**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Actively try to understand the buying experience from the prospect's perspective. Consider what they care about, their challenges, and why they might choose you or a competitor.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Top performers are effective at taking the perspective of their buyers. Empathy helps you anticipate their thoughts, concerns, and decision-making process, allowing for more relevant and impactful communication.

**6. We need to create value through our questions**
*   **Step-by-step process:** When a prospect states a problem, resist the urge to immediately offer a solution. Instead, ask deeper, insightful questions that help them explore the problem's implications and potential impact.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Like a psychologist, you guide the prospect to uncover their own insights and the depth of their problem. This process of self-discovery makes them feel heard and validates the problem, increasing their perceived need for a solution.

**7. "No" isn't bad**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Embrace "no" as a natural part of the process. Actively seek to disqualify prospects who are not a good fit as early as possible.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** A significant portion of prospects will never be a good fit. By quickly identifying these non-fits, you save time and reduce wasted effort. It also removes pressure from the prospect ("I'm not sure if this is a fit...").

**8. If you feel it, say it**
*   **Step-by-step process:** If you sense something is off (e.g., distraction, disinterest, hesitation), address it directly and gently.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Transparency and directness can disarm hidden objections or unspoken issues. It shows honesty and creates an environment where the prospect feels comfortable being honest in return. Often, they will re-engage more fully once the unspoken issue is addressed.

**9. Get deep into their challenges**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Think like a doctor, not a salesperson. Go beyond surface-level problems to understand the underlying core issues and implications. Use effective questions to "dig deeper."
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Analogous to an iceberg, prospects only show the tip of their problems. Effective questioning uncovers the hidden, more impactful challenges, allowing for a more comprehensive and valuable solution.

**10. Tie those challenges to value**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Once you've thoroughly explored their challenges, ask questions that quantify the potential value or impact of solving those challenges.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Prospects are driven by outcomes and benefits. By having *them* articulate the financial or tangible value of resolving their issues (e.g., increased revenue, profitability, savings, improved well-being), you make the solution's worth concrete and personally relevant. It becomes "their number," increasing ownership and buy-in.

**11. Make it a two-way dialogue**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Ensure the conversation is a continuous back-and-forth, with roughly equal speaking time for both parties. Avoid monologuing.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** People are most engaged when they are speaking. A two-way dialogue keeps the prospect actively involved, preventing them from mentally checking out. This frequent engagement also creates "mini-buys" throughout the conversation, making the final decision feel natural.

**12. Budget comes later**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Postpone discussions about price, cost, or budget until after you have thoroughly understood their challenges and tied those challenges to specific, quantified value.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** Discussing budget too early, before the value is established, positions your offering as a commodity. By delaying, you ensure the prospect understands the potential return on investment, making the price seem like a worthwhile investment rather than just an expense.

**13. Feedback loops**
*   **Step-by-step process:** Throughout the conversation, especially after presenting information or explaining something, use small, re-engaging questions to check for understanding and continued interest.
*   **Psychology/Principle:** These "mini-questions" (like "Does that make sense?") act as micro-commitments, continuously pulling the prospect back into the conversation and confirming alignment. They prevent assumptions, allow for clarification, and build momentum, making the final "close" feel like a natural next step rather than a pressured decision.

    *   **Application:** If someone seems disengaged or skeptical.
    *   **Script Example:** "I'm sensing a little hesitation when I mention the technology. Is there something about it that sounds too good to be true, or a particular concern you have?" (Addresses skepticism directly).

9.  **Get Deep Into Their Challenges:**
    *   **Application:** Don't just hear "I have trouble sleeping." Ask *why* it's a problem.
    *   **Script Example:** "When you say you're tired, what does that feel like throughout your day? Does it affect your focus, your mood, your patience? What would a good night's sleep consistently allow you to do that you can't do now?"

10. **Tie Those Challenges to Value:**
    *   **Application:** Quantify the impact of solving their problem with the patches.
    *   **Script Example:** "If you were able to finally get consistent, restful sleep, what would that mean for you in terms of your energy, your productivity, or even just enjoying your hobbies more? What's the value of feeling truly rested every day?" This gets them to think about the *benefit* in their own terms.

11. **Make it a Two-Way Dialogue:**
    *   **Application:** After explaining how a patch works, pause and ask.
    *   **Script Example:** "So, the technology in the patches essentially works by stimulating your nerve endings to send signals to your brain to [e.g., promote relaxation]. Does that explanation make sense, or would you like me to clarify anything?"

12. **Budget Comes Later:**
    *   **Application:** Don't lead with patch prices. Build the value first.
    *   **Script Example:** After tying challenges to value (e.g., better sleep means more energy, less pain means more activity), you can say: "Given the potential impact of getting [better sleep/pain relief], what kind of investment would you consider reasonable to achieve those results?" or "Based on the value we've identified, these solutions typically range from [e.g., $50-$150] per month depending on your specific needs. Where does that sound like it might fit for you?"

13. **Feedback Loops:**
    *   **Application:** Use these throughout any explanation or discussion.
    *   **Script Example:** "Does that resonate with your experience?" "Am I understanding your situation correctly?" "Does that sound like something you'd be open to exploring?" "Before we move on, are there any questions about that?" "What would you like to do next?" (For the natural close).
