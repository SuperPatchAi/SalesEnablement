### The Most POWERFUL Sales Closing Technique of All-Time

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=niLA7dJNM90](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=niLA7dJNM90)

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
The primary sales concept/technique being taught is the **Assumptive Close**.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
**Framework/Technique Name:** Assumptive Close

**Step-by-step process:**
1.  **Adopt an "Assumptive" Mindset:** Believe that the prospect will eventually buy your product/service, assuming it's a good fit and solves their problem. Your mindset should be "when," not "if."
2.  **Avoid Passivity:** Do not ask direct "yes/no" questions that put all control in the prospect's hands (e.g., "Will you buy my product?").
3.  **Avoid Aggression:** Do not demand an immediate decision or pressure the prospect excessively (e.g., "You need to make a decision on this right now!"). This will scare them away.
4.  **Use Assumptive Language & Questions:** Frame your questions and statements around the *assumption* that the sale will occur, focusing on logistical or preference-based choices. This steers the conversation toward implementation rather than decision-making on the core purchase.
5.  **Integrate throughout the Sales Process:** Apply this mindset and questioning technique from the very first discovery call through to the demo and final close.

**The psychology/principle behind why it works:**
The Assumptive Close works because it shifts the salesperson's mindset from uncertainty ("if they will buy") to certainty ("when they will buy"). This belief subtly impacts the language and questions used, framing the conversation as if the purchase is already a foregone conclusion. By offering choices about *how* or *when* to proceed, rather than *whether* to proceed, it psychologically moves the prospect past the initial "buy/don't buy" decision point and into the "how/when" logistics. It avoids giving the prospect an easy "no" and instead directs them toward an implied "yes" through alternative choices. This puts the salesperson in control of the next steps without being aggressive.

### 3. EXACT SCRIPTS & WORD TRACKS

**Assumptive Discovery/Closing Questions:**

*   "What day works better for delivery, Monday or Tuesday?"
*   "How many total licenses will you need?"
*   "What day would you like our technical team to begin implementation with your IT manager?"
*   "When would you like to hang out again, Monday or Tuesday of next week?" (Personal example)

**Assumptive Statements/Framing:**

*   "When you sign the order, this is what the implementation process will look like."
*   "Let's meet next Tuesday to review the pricing."

**Passive Questions to Avoid:**

*   "Will you buy my product?"
*   "Would you like to buy my product or service?"
*   "Can we meet next Tuesday?"

**Aggressive Statements to Avoid:**

*   "You need to make a decision on this right now."

### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING
The video **does not explicitly discuss objection handling** in terms of specific common objections and recommended responses. Instead, the focus is on adopting the assumptive close technique to guide the conversation and prevent "no" answers or objections related to the core purchase decision by assuming the sale will occur. The implicit goal is to preempt objections by confidently moving toward the next steps.

### 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  **"When, Not If" Mindset:** Approach every sales interaction with the belief that the prospect *will* buy if your product is a good fit. This fundamental assumption will naturally guide your language and questions.
2.  **Avoid Extremes (Passive/Aggressive):** Don't be so passive that you give all control to the prospect (e.g., asking "Will you buy?"), and don't be so aggressive that you scare them away (e.g., demanding an immediate decision).
3.  **Offer Assumptive Choices:** Instead of asking *if* they want to buy or take the next step, ask *when* or *how* they want to proceed by offering specific options (e.g., "Which day works better, A or B?").
4.  **Integrate Assumptive Language Early:** Start using assumptive questions and statements from the very first discovery call, not just at the end when you're trying to close. This sets the tone for a forward-moving conversation.
5.  **Control the Narrative:** The assumptive close allows you to maintain control of the sales process by guiding the prospect toward logistical decisions rather than open-ended purchase decisions, fostering a sense of progress.

### 6. SUPER PATCH APPLICATION

Applying the Assumptive Close to selling Super Patch wellness products:

1.  **Initial Discovery/Consultation:**
    *   Instead of: "Are you interested in trying a Super Patch for your pain?"
    *   Use: **"Based on what you've shared about your discomfort, when would be the best day next week for you to start feeling relief with the Super Patch, Monday or Tuesday?"** (Assumes they'll start, offers a choice of start date.)
    *   Instead of: "Do you want to know more about how these patches work?"
    *   Use: **"When we dive into the science behind the Super Patch technology, which aspect would you like to understand first: how it helps with pain, or how it improves sleep?"** (Assumes they want to learn, guides the discussion.)

2.  **Product Recommendation/Pricing:**
    *   Instead of: "Would you like to purchase a pack of these patches?"
    *   Use: **"Considering your needs, which pack size will work best for you to get started – the 30-day supply or the 90-day supply for sustained results?"** (Assumes they're buying, offers choice of quantity/duration.)
    *   Instead of: "Can I send you the pricing information?"
    *   Use: **"I can email you the link to complete your order, or would you prefer I walk you through the checkout process now?"** (Assumes they're ready to order, offers choice of method.)

3.  **Onboarding/Follow-up (after initial purchase):**
    *   Instead of: "Do you think you'll apply the patch every day?"
    *   Use: **"Now that you have your Super Patch, what time of day will you typically apply it for maximum benefit – first thing in the morning, or right before bed?"** (Assumes daily use, helps establish routine.)
    *   Instead of: "Let me know if you have any questions about using the patches."
    *   Use: **"After you've had a chance to try the patches for a few days, when would you like to reconnect to discuss your experience and ensure you're getting the most out of them – Thursday afternoon or Friday morning?"** (Assumes positive experience, schedules proactive follow-up.)

4.  **Team Building/Recruitment (for direct sales):**

    *   Use: **"When you decide to join our Super Patch team, which onboarding session would you like to attend – the one on Tuesday or the one on Saturday?"** (Assumes they'll join, offers choice of training time.)
    *   Instead of: "Can I share some information about our compensation plan?"
    *   Use: **"Once you see how rewarding it is to share Super Patch, how quickly do you envision reaching your first commission goal – within 30 days or 60 days?"** (Assumes success, frames future goals.)

### Best Sales Strategies on How To Close More Sales

**Source:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cMKmIU5Scxc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cMKmIU5Scxc)

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE

**Main Topic & Technique Name:** Continuous Closing (or Holistic Closing)

**Definition:** The primary sales concept taught is that "closing doesn't just happen at the end of a sales meeting. You're actually beginning your close the moment you meet someone and shake their hand and you're closing throughout the entire conversation until you eventually ask for the business at the end." This approach emphasizes doing "all the heavy lifting" upfront and throughout the conversation to make the final ask easy.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework/Technique 1: Be Better By Being Different**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Identify Differentiation (Self-Reflection):** Before interacting with prospects, consciously think about what makes you, your product, or your service stand out from the competition.
        *   *Examples given:* "Do you offer different case studies of people that you helped in the past...?" "Do you have better communication skills or are you easier to work with...?" "Is your product and service actually better? Is there a significant improvement like a 2X, 3X times better or are you 30% better, 50% better...?"
    2.  **Articulate Uniqueness:** Formulate clear and compelling ways to describe these differentiators to prospects.
    3.  **Incorporate Proactively:** Integrate these unique selling propositions into your pitch from the very beginning of the sales meeting.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:** "People naturally perceive different as better. It may not always be true, different doesn't mean you actually provide a better service, but in the minds of people, people do believe that... someone who's different can be better." By establishing yourself as different and implicitly better early on, you create a stronger perception of value, making the final closing stage significantly smoother.

**Framework/Technique 2: Get Clients to Invest Emotionally (and Turn "Nice-to-Have" into "Must-Have")**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Deep Problem Identification:** Instead of just showcasing features, delve into the core problems and pains your prospects are experiencing.
    2.  **Quantify the Cost of Inaction:** Clearly articulate the emotional and logical costs (time, money, employee happiness, missed opportunities) of *not* adopting your solution.
        *   *Example given (Software):* A student pitching software was advised to highlight how it could "literally save months of their employees' time" and translate that into "tens of thousands of dollars saved," leading to happier employees who can "do the job that they were hired to do."
    3.  **Reposition as Essential:** Shift your offering from a mere "nice-to-have" to an absolute "must-have" by emphasizing the critical impact it has on solving the identified problems and preventing significant losses.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:** "People are always buying emotionally... People make their decisions emotionally even in B2B settings and they're going to justify that decision logically." By connecting your solution to deep-seated emotional drivers (e.g., fear of loss, desire for relief, improving quality of life) and then providing logical justifications (e.g., ROI, efficiency), you build a powerful case that resonates deeply with the prospect.

**Framework/Technique 3: Handle Objections Early**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Identify Common Objections:** Know the typical objections that arise in your sales cycle for your product/service.
    2.  **Proactive Discussion:** Address these common objections at the beginning or in the middle of the conversation, rather than waiting until the very end.
    3.  **Qualify Effectively:** Use early objection handling to determine if the prospect is genuinely qualified and a good fit, preventing you from investing too much time in deals that won't close.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:** "If you do not handle the objections in the beginning or early on... you're going to go through the entire sales process... and at the end you might find that the prospect's not even qualified to buy because you never went through the objections..." By confronting potential roadblocks early, you eliminate false hopes, save valuable time for both parties, and build transparency, making the path to a positive close much smoother.

**Framework/Technique 4: Understand How Prospects Want To Be Closed**

*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Directly Ask About Their Process:** At an appropriate point (often towards the end of an initial meeting), ask the prospect about their internal decision-making and approval process.
    2.  **Map Out Key Steps:** Collaboratively list all the internal meetings, approvals, demonstrations, and any other steps required for a decision to be made at their company.
    3.  **Establish a Mutual Timeline:** For each identified step, work with the prospect to assign realistic (even approximate) dates or timeframes. This creates a shared roadmap for the deal.
    4.  **Confirm & Document:** Verbally confirm each step and timeline, and actively take notes.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:** Different clients, especially in larger organizations, have unique buying cycles. By asking *how they want to be closed*, you demonstrate respect for their process, avoid pushing them into an uncomfortable or unrealistic closing scenario, and establish a partnership. This approach removes sales pressure, builds trust, and ensures both parties are aligned on the path forward, leading to a smoother and more predictable close. "They told you how they want to be closed."

### 3. EXACT SCRIPTS & WORD TRACKS

*   **To Understand Their Internal Closing Process:**
    *   "Look, um, you know, it seems like everything is going great so far and seems like we're a good fit to work with each other. Now, I'm just curious to understand, you know, if we were to get this deal done, what would it look like on your end? You know, what would need to happen internally at your company for us to move forward with something like this?"

*   **To Establish Timelines for Each Step:**
    *   (After they outline a step, e.g., "talk to my boss"): "And just curious to know when that meeting is actually going to happen?"
    *   (To confirm a date/timeframe): "Okay, so I can expect it to be done this Friday?"
    *   (To schedule a meeting): "You might if I go ahead and schedule a meeting for that time? And then we can, you know, further discuss about that?"

*   **To Shorten the Sales Cycle (if appropriate):**
    *   "You're saying that you want to do this three weeks from now but why would it need to be done three weeks from now? Do you think we can get it done within the week? This way we can save some time for both of us."

### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING

**Technique:** Handle Objections Early (Framework 3).

*   **Common objections mentioned (examples):**
    *   "They just don't have the budget."
    *   The implied objection of "it's just a nice-to-have, not a must-have."
*   **Recommended responses/reframe:**
    *   The video emphasizes proactive qualification. For budget, the advice is to discover this early on to avoid "going through your entire sales process... and you find out at the end, the prospect doesn't even have the budget to buy." The reframe is to save time and ensure you're working with qualified prospects.
    *   For the "nice-to-have" objection, the response is to reframe the product/service as a "must-have" by quantifying the costs of *not* buying (time, money, employee morale) and appealing to emotional drivers (Framework 2).

### 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1.  **Always Be Closing (Holistically):** View every interaction as part of the closing process, building trust and value continuously from start to finish.
2.  **Be Uniquely Better:** Clearly articulate and demonstrate what differentiates you and your offering from competitors to establish perceived superiority.
3.  **Sell Emotionally, Justify Logically:** Tap into prospects' deepest pains and desires first, then provide logical data and ROI to reinforce their emotional decision.
4.  **Confront Objections Early:** Don't delay addressing concerns. Proactively clarify doubts and qualify prospects upfront to avoid wasted effort and increase closing efficiency.
5.  **Collaborate on the Closing Process:** Ask prospects about their preferred buying journey and co-create a step-by-step timeline. This ensures alignment, respects their autonomy, and builds a predictable path to commitment.

**Main Topic:** Relationship Building for Direct Sales Success
**Technique:** The "3+3+3=3000" Model

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework/Technique Name: The "3+3+3=3000" Model**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Identify 3 Current Customers:** These are people who have already purchased from you.
    2.  **Identify 3 Potential Customers:** These are people who might be interested in your products/services in the future.
    3.  **Identify 3 Potential Teammates:** These are people you would love to recruit to join your direct sales team.
    4.  **Connect Daily:** The challenge is to connect with these 9 individuals *every day* (you can take a day off here and there).
    5.  **Focus on Connection, Not Selling:** The initial connections should be genuine, conversational, and not immediately about pitching your product or business.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:**
    *   **Consistency:** Daily action builds momentum and makes relationship-building a habit.
    *   **Volume:** 9 connections daily results in over 3,000 connections annually, significantly expanding your network and potential business opportunities exponentially.
    *   **Authenticity:** By focusing on genuine interactions and getting to know people as individuals, you build rapport and trust, which are foundational for long-term customer relationships and team growth. People are more likely to buy from and join people they know, like, and trust.

**Framework/Technique Name: Building and Utilizing a "List of 100"**
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  **Create Your Initial List:** Start with a list of 100 people you know in your business (current customers, prospects, potential teammates, general network).
    2.  **Map Out Your Week:** Plan which 3+3+3 individuals you will connect with each day from your list.
    3.  **Continual Expansion:** Actively grow and expand this list by meeting new people through events, parties, and daily interactions (e.g., meeting moms at the playground).
    4.  **Regular Updates:** As you connect and your network grows, consistently update and add to your list.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:**
    *   **Structured Prospecting:** Provides a tangible and manageable list to work from, preventing analysis paralysis.
    *   **Dynamic Growth:** Acknowledges that networking is ongoing and your list should evolve with your business growth and new relationships.
    *   **Accountability:** Mapping out your week creates a plan for consistent outreach.

1.  **Prioritize Relationships:** Direct sales success isn't about selling; it's about connecting with people and building genuine relationships.

5.  **Utilize a Dynamic Prospect List:** Maintain and continually grow a "List of 100" (or more) contacts, using it to strategically plan your daily relationship-building efforts.

## VIDEO ANALYSIS

### 1. MAIN TOPIC & TECHNIQUE
**Main Topic:** Practical field-tested strategies for direct sales representatives to enhance confidence, focus, and closing rates by effectively handling objections, segmenting prospects, and creating urgency.

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**A. Handling Negativity and Criticism (The "I Guess We'll See" Method)**
*   **Framework/Technique Name:** The "I Guess We'll See" Response
*   **Step-by-step process:**
    1.  When a prospect starts being critical, "poking holes" in your direct selling opportunity, or expressing skepticism.
    2.  Instead of arguing or becoming defensive (which "didn't serve me very well"), pause.
    3.  Respond with the exact phrase: "I guess we'll see."
    4.  Deliver this with a "nice big smile" and maintain composure.
    5.  Walk away from the interaction.
*   **The psychology/principle behind why it works:**
    *   **Maintains Positivity & Power:** It prevents you from giving away your energy and power to the prospect.
    *   **Defuses Argumentation:** It cuts off the opportunity for an argument. There's "nothing that they really can say for that."
    *   **Earns Respect:** Most prospects who are critical are just trying to make you "dance that worrisome dance." When you don't engage, they "may not like it, but they'll definitely respect you."
    *   **Preserves Self-Esteem:** Allows you to "walk away with your head held high" and feel "more empowered and positive."

**B. Prospect Segmentation (Two Specific Sales Funnels)**
*   **Framework/Technique Name:** Dual Sales Funnel Approach
*   **Step-by-step process:**

### 2. KEY FRAMEWORKS & STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

**Framework/Technique Name:** Conversation Aikido

**Step-by-step process:**

1.  **Receive the Statement/Objection:** The customer presents a statement, question, or objection (e.g., "Your product is too expensive," "You're always late").
2.  **Acknowledge and Understand (Don't Answer):** Instead of immediately providing an answer or counter-argument, the salesperson expresses understanding and paraphrases the customer's statement or the feeling behind it. The key is to convey empathy and show you've heard them.
3.  **Ask an Open-Ended Question:** Immediately follow the acknowledgment with an open-ended question designed to uncover the *real*, deeper issue, need, or interest driving their initial statement.

**The psychology/principle behind why it works:**

*   **Avoids "Stupidity" (Emotional Overload):** The speaker uses a brain model to illustrate that when emotions rise (e.g., due to feeling attacked or argued with), the logical thinking part of the brain "disconnects." Direct answers or arguments raise emotions, making the other person less receptive to logic. "The higher emotion goes, the lower thinking goes." (0:55, 2:43-3:00)
*   **Uncovers the Real Issue:** Surface-level objections (like "too expensive") are often not the true underlying problem. By avoiding a direct answer and instead asking an open question, you create an opportunity to discover what's *really* driving their hesitation, concern, or need. "When someone says your product is too expensive, that's not what their real issue is." (2:15-2:18)
*   **Uses Their Energy Against Them (Aikido):** Like in martial arts, where an opponent's force is used against them, Conversation Aikido uses the energy of the objection to move the conversation forward constructively. You "go towards the punch, dodge it, and look and you're seeing the world in the same direction as the person who's attacking you." (4:28-4:54)
*   **Fosters Connection and Trust:** By demonstrating active listening and a genuine desire to understand their perspective, you build rapport and trust. "I understand and accepting the energy that's coming from the other person and then giving back an open question." (7:00-7:08)
*   **Shuts Down Argumentation:** It prevents the conversation from devolving into a "no, it's not" debate, which yields no valuable information and damages the relationship. "By giving the answer, we shut down the possibility of hearing what's really going on." (6:23-6:29)
